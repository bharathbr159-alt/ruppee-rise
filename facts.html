📚 Investment Basics

Fundamental truths every investor must understand before putting money anywhere

📉
50% Loss Reality
If your investment falls 50%, you need a 100% gain just to break even. Protecting capital is as important as growing it.
₹1L → falls to ₹50K → needs to double to get back to ₹1L
🔥
Inflation Eats Wealth
At 6% inflation, ₹1 lakh today is worth only ₹74,000 in 5 years. Money sitting idle loses value silently.
FD at 7% barely beats 6% inflation. Always invest to stay ahead.
📈
Compounding Power
Starting early is more powerful than investing large amounts later. Time in market beats timing the market.
₹5,000/month for 30 years at 12% = ₹1.7 Crore. Start 10 years late = only ₹50L
🧠
Investor Psychology
Most investors buy high (greed) and sell low (fear) — the exact opposite of what they should. SIPs remove emotion from investing.
Automate your SIP and never check it during a market crash
🦁
Risk vs Reward
Higher returns always come with higher risk. FD = low risk, low return. Stocks = high risk, high return. Match risk to your age.
Rule: Bond % = Your Age. At 35 → 35% bonds, 65% equity
🎯
80-20 Portfolio Rule
Keep 80% in Indian investments and 20% in global investments for balanced risk. India is only 4% of world market — diversify!
20% global = exposure to Apple, Microsoft, Nvidia growth

📜 Bonds — Fixed Income Investing

Everything you need to know about lending money to companies and earning fixed returns

🏦
What is a Bond?
You lend money to a company or government. They pay you fixed interest (coupon) regularly and return your principal on maturity date. Minimum: ₹10,000 on IndiaBonds.
Think of it as giving a loan — you're the bank!
🛡️
Bondholders Get Paid First
If a company goes bankrupt, bondholders are paid before shareholders. Senior secured bondholders are first in line — company assets protect them.
Equity holders get whatever is left — often nothing
🪜
Bond Laddering Strategy
Spread bonds across different maturity dates — 1yr, 3yr, 5yr. This gives regular cash flow and reduces timing risk.
₹3L (1yr) + ₹3L (3yr) + ₹4L (5yr) = income every year
👨‍👧
Start Early — Even for Children
A ₹50,000 portfolio started for a 14-year-old at 12% return becomes ₹2.2L by age 21 — more than many starting salaries!
Financial literacy + early investing = generational wealth
🏠
Bonds Beat Real Estate Income
Real estate gives 2-3% rental yield after maintenance and taxes. Bonds give 8-11% with zero maintenance. Plus bonds are liquid — you can't sell half a flat!
₹50L in bonds = ₹4-5L/year. ₹50L flat = ₹1-1.5L/year rent after costs
🔄
Repo Rate & Bonds
When RBI raises repo rate, new bonds offer higher interest but your old bonds lose market value. Once you buy, your coupon rate stays fixed regardless.
Don't panic about repo rate changes after buying — returns are locked in
💸
TDS on Bond Interest
Bond interest has 10% TDS. If income below ₹12L, claim it back via ITR. Pro tip: gift bonds to retired parents — their lower tax slab saves money legally.
Always file ITR to claim bond TDS refunds
⚠️
Harshad Mehta 1992 Warning
In 1992, Harshad Mehta manipulated markets causing a massive crash. No digital oversight existed. This led to SEBI's formation. Always use regulated platforms.
If returns seem too good to be true — they are. Avoid unregulated schemes.

Should You Buy Physical Gold? Think Again.

Gold has surged from ₹4,000 to ₹7,000+/gram but buying physical gold hurts India's economy and your own returns. When you buy gold, India imports more — increasing dollar demand, weakening INR, worsening the trade deficit. There are smarter ways to get gold exposure.

🚫
Physical Gold Hurts Economy
India imports most gold. Every gram bought increases India's dollar outflow — weakening rupee and increasing inflation for everyone.
India has ₹100+ lakh crore in idle physical gold — a "dead economy" trap
💸
Hidden Costs of Gold Jewellery
Making charges (10-25%) + GST (3%) + storage costs = you lose 15-30% immediately. Gold jewellery as investment is a very bad idea.
You'll rarely recover making charges when selling jewellery
📦
Gold Generates Zero Income
Physical gold in a locker earns nothing — no dividends, no interest. It only gains if prices rise. Meanwhile your money isn't working.
₹5L in SIP vs ₹5L in physical gold over 10 years — SIP wins by far
✅
Better: Sovereign Gold Bonds
SGBs give gold price gains + 2.5% annual interest + zero capital gains tax if held till maturity. No making charges, no storage needed.
Best of all worlds — gold exposure + income + tax benefit
🛢️
Oil Price → Inflation → You
India imports 88% crude oil. Oil $60 → $110 + INR falling = import bill doubled. Higher petrol → higher transport → everything gets expensive.
School fees up 30%, groceries up — middle class loses disposable income
🏛️
Possible Gold Import Ban
As INR weakens toward ₹120-130 by 2029, India may restrict gold imports to protect forex reserves — reducing physical gold liquidity.
Another reason to hold Gold ETFs instead of physical gold

🦁 Understanding Investment Risk

The lion in a cage analogy — risk exists everywhere, what matters is how well it's contained

🦁

Imagine a lion inside a cage

The lion is dangerous but the cage controls the risk. You can stand near the cage (investing) without getting hurt — as long as the cage is strong. But enter the cage (reckless investing) and you're in real danger. Risk exists in all investments. The key is how well it's contained.

🚶
100 Metres Away — No Investing
You're safe but money isn't growing. Inflation eats savings silently. This is keeping money in savings account or under the mattress.
0% risk but inflation still wins. Not investing IS a risk.
🏛️
Outside the Cage — Bonds & FDs
Protected by strong bars (credit ratings + debenture trustees). Even if company fails, secured bondholders get paid from assets first.
8-11% returns. Cage = credit rating + trustee + SEBI oversight
🚪
Cage Door Open — Mutual Funds
Door is open but a trained handler (fund manager) is present. Risk exists but it's professionally managed and diversified across many companies.
12-15% potential. Handler = fund manager + SEBI regulation
⚡
Inside the Cage — Direct Equity
You're directly with the lion. Equity holders are LAST paid in bankruptcy. Stock can go to zero. But also potential for 10x returns.
Last priority in bankruptcy. Maximum risk = maximum reward.
🔓
Broken Cage — Unrated Bonds
Unsecured, unrated bonds with no debenture trustee = broken cage. Company default risk can strike without warning. Avoid completely.
No rating + no security = gambling, not investing
📹
CCTV & Police — SEBI Regulations
Post-1992 Harshad Mehta scam, SEBI created strict market oversight — like adding CCTV and police to the lion's enclosure. Markets are far safer today.
1992: No oversight → market crash. 2024: SEBI monitors everything.

📖 Financial Terms Explained

All the jargon decoded in plain simple language

SIP
Systematic Investment Plan — invest fixed amount monthly in mutual funds automatically
DEMAT Account
Electronic account for holding stocks, bonds and ETFs in digital form
ETF
Exchange Traded Fund — basket of assets that trades on stock exchange like a single share
Coupon Rate
Fixed annual interest % paid on a bond — your guaranteed income from the bond
YTM
Yield to Maturity — total return you get if you hold the bond till its end date
Credit Rating
AAA = safest company, D = defaulted. Never invest in bonds below BBB rating
Debenture Trustee
Regulated entity that monitors bond issuers and protects bondholder interests
Bond Laddering
Buying bonds at different maturity dates to get regular income and reduce risk
Repo Rate
Rate at which RBI lends to banks — the base rate that controls all interest rates in India
GIFT City (IFSC)
Gujarat International Finance Tech-City — India's regulated hub for investing in global markets
LRS
Liberalized Remittance Scheme — RBI allows Indians to send up to ₹20L abroad per year for investing
DTAA
Double Tax Avoidance Agreement — prevents paying tax twice on same income in two countries
Expense Ratio
Annual fee charged by mutual fund for managing your money — lower is always better
Sovereign Gold Bond
Govt bond linked to gold price — gives gold gains + 2.5% interest + no capital gains tax
Fractional Shares
Buy a portion of a share — invest ₹500 in Amazon without buying a full share
Fiscal Deficit
When govt spends more than it earns — limits ability to control inflation
Dead Economy
Money trapped in non-productive assets like physical gold/real estate — slows economic growth
Beta
How much a stock moves vs the market. Beta > 1 = more volatile. Beta < 1 = more stable
TDS
Tax Deducted at Source — 10% auto-deducted on bond interest. Claim back via ITR filing
Senior Bond
First priority in repayment if company goes bankrupt — safest type of bond
